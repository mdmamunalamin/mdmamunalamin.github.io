NIH-Style Contributions to Science

This page follows the NIH biosketch (Contribution to Science) format, highlighting scientific advances, personal contributions, and representative products. Interactive filters allow cross-cutting exploration across themes.

Contribution 1: Genetic Drivers of Alzheimer’s Disease and White Matter Integrity

I elucidated how genetic risk factors for Alzheimer’s disease (AD), particularly APOE4 and microglial risk genes, disrupt white matter integrity, myelination, and immune responses. Using diffusion MRI, histology, and transgenic mouse models, my work provided mechanistic links between genetic risk, network disconnection, and cognitive decline.

Contribution 2: Connectomics and Network Abnormalities in Brain Disorders

I applied advanced diffusion MRI, fixel-based analysis, and graph theory to identify subtle disruptions in structural connectivity across neurological and psychiatric conditions, including Alzheimer’s disease, schizophrenia, ADHD, and sensory loss.

Contribution 3: Neuroinflammation as a Therapeutic Target in Psychiatric and Neurological Disorders

I demonstrated that immune dysregulation contributes to psychiatric illness and that pharmacological modulation of inflammation and oxidative stress can protect brain structure and function in both human and animal models.

Contribution 4: Vitamin D as a Modulator of Hippocampal Plasticity and Cognitive Resilience

I identified vitamin D deficiency as a modifiable biological factor associated with hippocampal atrophy, impaired synaptic plasticity, and disrupted brain connectivity, integrating clinical imaging with mechanistic animal studies.

Contribution 5: Neuropharmacology and Oxidative–Inflammatory Mechanisms

I established oxidative–inflammatory pathways as therapeutic targets in neurodevelopmental and neurodegenerative disorders, demonstrating behavioral and molecular rescue using pharmacological and antioxidant interventions.
