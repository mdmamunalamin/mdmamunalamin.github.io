About Me

I am an Assistant Research Professor of Medical and Molecular Genetics at the Stark Neurosciences Research Institute, Indiana University School of Medicine. My work focuses on the cellular, molecular, and genetic mechanisms that drive neurodegenerative diseases, particularly Alzheimer's disease, with an emphasis on white matter pathology, myelination, and brain network integrity.

Trained across Bangladesh, Germany, and Australia—with a PhD from The University of Queensland, an M.Sc. from Ludwig-Maximilian University Munich, and earlier degrees from Jahangirnagar University—I advance translational neuroscience that integrates molecular pathology with brain network organization while championing diversity in the field.

Research

Alzheimer's Disease & Neurodegeneration

My research investigates the cellular, molecular, and genetic mechanisms of neurodegenerative diseases, with a primary focus on Alzheimer's disease pathology. I use genetically engineered mouse models of Aβ amyloidosis to dissect how microglia, oligodendrocytes, and myelin integrity shape disease progression and cognitive outcomes.

White Matter & Structural Connectivity

I integrate structural and diffusion MRI, including advanced diffusion models such as NODDI, to characterize white matter microstructure and structural connectivity. These data link distributed network changes to behavioral performance and histopathological readouts.

Hippocampus & Memory

My work connects hippocampal volume, spine density, nitric oxide signaling, and long‑range connectivity with spatial learning and memory in both human and animal studies, aiming to define circuit‑level substrates of cognitive resilience and vulnerability.

Technical Expertise

  • Behavioral phenotyping and spatial memory testing systems
  • Semi‑automated immunohistochemistry image analysis with machine learning
  • Convolutional neural networks for brain segmentation
  • Structural MRI, diffusion imaging, and NODDI analysis pipelines
