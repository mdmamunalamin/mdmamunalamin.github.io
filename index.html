About

I am an Assistant Research Professor in the Department of Medical & Molecular Genetics at Indiana University School of Medicine. My research focuses on the molecular, cellular, and systems-level mechanisms underlying neurodegenerative disorders, particularly Alzheimer’s disease.

My work integrates neuropathology, diffusion MRI, and network neuroscience to understand how white matter degeneration contributes to cognitive decline. I have trained internationally across Bangladesh, Germany, and Australia.

Research Focus

Alzheimer’s Disease

Mechanisms of microglial activation, oligodendrocyte dysfunction, and myelin pathology.

White Matter Connectivity

Diffusion MRI and advanced microstructural modeling linked to cognition and pathology.

Hippocampus & Memory

Structural and signaling mechanisms underlying learning, memory, and resilience.

Teaching & Mentoring

University-level teaching in neuroscience, pharmacology, and pharmaceutical sciences, with extensive mentoring of undergraduate and graduate trainees.
