About Me

I am an Assistant Research Professor of Medical and Molecular Genetics at the Stark Neurosciences Research Institute, Indiana University School of Medicine. My work focuses on the cellular, molecular, and genetic mechanisms that drive neurodegenerative diseases, particularly Alzheimer's disease, with emphasis on white matter pathology, myelination, and brain network integrity.

Trained across Bangladesh, Germany, and Australia, I pursue translational neuroscience that integrates molecular pathology with large-scale brain network organization while championing diversity in science.

Research

Alzheimer's Disease & Neurodegeneration

I study molecular and cellular mechanisms underlying Alzheimer’s disease using genetically engineered mouse models, with a focus on microglia, oligodendrocytes, and myelin pathology.

White Matter & Connectivity

My work integrates diffusion MRI and advanced modeling (including NODDI) to link microstructural white matter changes to cognitive outcomes and histopathology.

Hippocampus & Memory

I investigate how hippocampal structure, nitric oxide signaling, and long-range connectivity contribute to learning, memory, and cognitive resilience.
