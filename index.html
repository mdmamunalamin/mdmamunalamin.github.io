About Me

My research investigates the cellular, molecular, and genetic mechanisms of neurodegenerative diseases, with a primary focus on Alzheimer's disease pathology. I employ an integrative approach combining advanced neuroimaging modalities—including structural MRI, diffusion MRI, and NODDI with comprehensive behavioral phenotyping and quantitative histological analyses to elucidate the contributions of white matter microstructure, oligodendrocyte-mediated myelination, and hippocampal circuitry to cognitive decline across disease progression.

Trained across Bangladesh, Germany, and Australia with a PhD from The University of Queensland, M.Sc. from Ludwig-Maximilian University Munich, and earlier degrees from Jahangirnagar University, I advance translational neuroscience that integrates molecular pathology with brain network organization while championing diversity in the field.

Research

Alzheimer's Disease & Neurodegeneration

Investigating how amyloid pathology, microglial function, and oligodendrocyte biology interact to shape white matter integrity and cognitive outcomes in mouse models of Aβ amyloidosis (5xFAD, APP-NL-G-F).

White Matter & Structural Connectivity

Examining how disruptions in myelination and structural connectivity contribute to early Alzheimer's disease using diffusion MRI and NODDI analyses.

Hippocampus & Memory

Linking hippocampal volume, spine density, nitric oxide signaling, and connectivity with spatial learning and memory in both human neuroimaging and animal models.

Technical Expertise

  • Behavioral phenotyping & spatial memory testing systems
  • Semi-automated immunohistochemistry image analysis with machine learning
  • Convolutional neural networks for brain segmentation
  • Structural MRI, diffusion imaging, and NODDI analysis pipelines
