About Me

I am a neuroscientist specializing in the cellular, molecular, and genetic mechanisms underlying neurodegenerative diseases, particularly Alzheimer's disease. My research integrates advanced neuroimaging techniques (structural MRI, diffusion imaging, NODDI), behavioral phenotyping, and histological analysis to understand how white matter integrity, myelination, and hippocampal circuitry contribute to cognitive decline.

Originally from Bangladesh, I completed my PhD at The University of Queensland (Australia), M.Sc. at Ludwig-Maximilian University (Germany), and earlier degrees at Jahangirnagar University (Bangladesh). I am committed to promoting diversity in neuroscience while advancing translational research that bridges molecular pathology with brain network organization.

Research

Alzheimer's Disease & Neurodegeneration

Investigating how amyloid pathology, microglial function, and oligodendrocyte biology interact to shape white matter integrity and cognitive outcomes in mouse models of Aβ amyloidosis (5xFAD, APP-NL-G-F).

White Matter & Structural Connectivity

Examining how disruptions in myelination and structural connectivity contribute to early Alzheimer's disease using diffusion MRI and NODDI analyses.

Hippocampus & Memory

Linking hippocampal volume, spine density, nitric oxide signaling, and connectivity with spatial learning and memory in both human neuroimaging and animal models.

Technical Expertise

  • Behavioral phenotyping & spatial memory testing systems
  • Semi-automated immunohistochemistry image analysis with machine learning
  • Convolutional neural networks for brain segmentation
  • Structural MRI, diffusion imaging, and NODDI analysis pipelines
